üìö Understanding the Even/Odd Probability System

  • How It Works: Player (Boy) always goes first on Chamber 1, 3, 5 (odd). Computer (Girl) goes on Chamber 2, 4, 6 (even).
  • Fair Game (50/50): Bullet is equally likely to be in ANY chamber. Both player and computer have equal 50% chance of getting shot.
  • Tweaked Game: The bullet is placed based on Even Probability setting:
    • Even Prob = 35% ‚Üí 35% chance bullet in EVEN chamber (computer shot) + 65% in ODD (player shot) = House Advantage
    • Even Prob = 65% ‚Üí 65% chance bullet in EVEN chamber (computer shot) + 35% in ODD (player shot) = Player Advantage
  • Key Insight: The probability tweak is nearly undetectable in short sessions because high variance masks the weighted distribution!

üéØ Fair Game vs Tweaked Game Comparison

  • Fair Game (50/50): Bullet equally likely in any chamber. Player wins ~50%, Computer wins ~50%. Over time, both break even.
  • Tweaked Game (e.g., 35/65): Bullet placed based on Even/Odd probability. With even_prob=35%, player wins only ~35% of games.
  • Mathematical Guarantee: Even + Odd = 100% always. Setting even_prob automatically sets odd_prob = (1 - even_prob).
  • House Advantage Formula: House edge = (Odd% - Even%) / 2. Example: (65% - 35%) / 2 = 15% house advantage.
  • Long-Term Impact: Fair game ‚Üí ‚Ç±0 expected profit/loss. Tweaked game ‚Üí steady house profit accumulation.
  • Empirical vs Theoretical: Over many plays, empirical win rate converges to theoretical probability (Law of Large Numbers).
